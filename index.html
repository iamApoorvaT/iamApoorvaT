About

I am a junior research assistant with a passion for understanding human behavior through a scientific lens. I completed my M.A. in Psychology from the University of Mumbai. At Monk Prayogshala, my work focuses on designing and implementing studies in the field of behavioral economics and social psychology.

My academic interests lie in the cognitive biases that influence decision-making and the application of research to real-world social challenges. I am dedicated to contributing to meaningful research that can inform policy and improve societal well-being.

Recent Research & Publications

Cognitive Load and Decision-Making in Financial Literacy: A Pilot Study

Monk Prayogshala Working Paper Series, No. 20 (2023)

This paper investigates the impact of cognitive load on financial decision-making among young adults in urban India. The findings suggest a significant correlation between information overload and sub-optimal choices.

The Power of Nudges: Encouraging Pro-Environmental Behavior

Under Review

An ongoing project exploring the effectiveness of behavioral nudges in promoting environmentally friendly actions. The study utilizes a randomized control trial to test different interventions in a real-world setting.
